What is ENDSPE ?

ENDSPE stands for Endangered Species. Our project aims at bringing some light over the imperilment of living creatures on Earth in a more global way than what we are used to see.


We want to make you visualize the threats on the whole biosphere, and not only on some highly mediatized species, like polar bears or koalas.

How does it work ?

On the main page, you can see the Tree of life, of phylogenic tree, split upon the Animal and Plant reign. You can choose which one you want to see on the left menu. It represents each and every species, grouped by taxinomic rank (a fancy word that stands for the species, subspecies, families, and so on that defines the order in the tree).


The color of the species in the tree represents their state of danger.


By clicking on a species' node, you can access to a quick definition of what it is, and follow a Wikipedia link to get more informations about it.


Special thanks to Mike Bostock for his radial dendrogram and his introduction to d3-fisheye.

Where does the data come from ?

The data used in the visualisation comes from the IUCN European Red List. The color coding follows the one used by the IUCN Red List Category and Criteria Guide, it is a great resource if you want to get a better understanding of the meaning behind each category.
